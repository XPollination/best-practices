# First Comment Strategy

## The Problem with "Link in Comments"

Most people treat the first comment as a link dump:
> "For anyone who wants to dig deeper â€” here's the article: [link]"

This is passive. It assumes the reader is already motivated enough to click. It provides no reason to click. It has no call to action.

## What the First Comment Should Do

The first comment is **part of the post**. It's the continuation, not an afterthought. It should:

1. **Add value beyond the post** â€” give a reason to click that the post didn't
2. **Include a clear CTA** â€” what should the reader do and why
3. **Address a specific audience** â€” not "anyone", but "if you're a CTO dealing with..."
4. **Create urgency or curiosity** â€” what will they learn/get that they can't get from the post alone

## Pattern

```
[1 sentence: who this is for + what they'll get]
[Link]
[Optional: 1 sentence on what's in the article that wasn't in the post]
```
