## The Problem

Thomas operates in DACH (German-speaking) but positions XPollination internationally (English website, English blog). LinkedIn audience is mixed. Should posts be in EN, DE, or both?

## Decision: One Post, English, With German Footer

### Why not two posts (EN + DE)?

LinkedIn's algorithm treats near-duplicate content as spam. Even translated, the second post gets suppressed. The effort of two posts yields less total reach than one well-crafted post.

### Why English as primary?

- Blog and website are English-first (DE available)
- B2B decision-makers in DACH read English professionally
- English reaches international network segments without friction
- Establishes thought-leadership voice consistently in one language

### Why a German footer?

German-speaking readers who scroll past English text get a hook in their native language. This is NOT a translation â€” it's a bridge. 3-4 sentences that:

1. Acknowledge German-speaking readers
2. Provide a standalone micro-insight (not just "read the English above")
3. Link to the German version of the content
